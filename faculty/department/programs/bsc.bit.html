Bachelor of Science in Business Information Technology

1.0 Programme Description

BSc. in Business Information Technology degree is to give students sound knowledge of Business Information Technology fundamentals and their applications. Students in this program are expected to acquire IT with Business skills and knowledge that includes programming, web development, database design, Information system, Service Marketing, E-Business, Network Security, Entrepreneurship, and Maintenance and Administration, hardware architecture and Operating Systems. Graduate from this degree can be employed both in private and public sectors as well as self-employment.

2.0 Admission Entry Requirements

2.1 Admission Requirement for Students Who Completed Form Six:

Two principal passes in the following subjects: History, Geography, Economics, Commerce, Accountancy, Book Keeping, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Advanced Mathematics, Agriculture, Computer Science or Nutrition.

2.2 Admission Requirement for Students Who Completed Diploma:

Diploma in Economics, Accountancy, Business Administration, Management, Marketing, Law, Information Communication Technology, Business Information Technology, Computer Science, Banking and Finance, Statistics, Telecommunications, Science with Education, Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Procurement, Public Administration or Accounting with an average of “B’’ or a minimum GPA of 3.0. OR Foundation Certificate of The OUT with a minimum GPA of 3.0.

3.0 Programme Objectives

  1. Enable students to seamlessly merge business insights with advanced technological expertise, so they understand how these two areas work together.

  2. Provide students with a comprehensive understanding of how information technology is strategically applied in diverse business contexts.

  3. Develop effective communication skills, allowing graduates to bridge the gap between technical and business teams within an organization.

  4. Cultivate problem-solving and decision-making abilities to address challenges at the intersection of business and technology, contributing to organizational success.
