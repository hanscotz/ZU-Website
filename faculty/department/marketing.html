Dear Students, General Publics and ZU Community

Welcome to the Marketing Department at Zanzibar University. Our department is dedicated to nurturing future marketing leaders through innovative teaching, practical experience, and strong industry engagement. We are excited to guide you through a transformative academic journey.

Department Background

The marketing department was founded in 1998 and currently provides a single undergraduate programme, the Bachelor of Business Administration in Marketing. A total of 67 pupils in all were enrolled in the programme for the 2023–2024 academic years. The MBA Marketing programme is the only postgraduate programme offered by the department. A new programme designed by the department is the Diploma in Tourism Management which is currently being evaluated by the Tanzania Commission for Universities (TCU).

The department is also anticipated to launch more postgraduate degrees, such as that of PhD in Marketing Management, a Master's degree in Marketing and Tourism, a Master's degree in Petroleum Management and Promotion, and a Master's degree in Tourism Management. However, the department is keen to provide a Bachelor of Science in Marketing and Information Technology, Bachelor of Business Administration in Digital Marketing, Bachelor of Innovation and Entrepreneurship, and Bachelor of Marketing and Tourism.

There are eight qualified academic staff members in the department, including three (3) PhD holders, four (4) currently enrolled in PhD programmes, and one (1) anticipated to register by the 2024–2025 academic year. Three (3) part-time lecturers, primarily 2 in Marketing and 4 in the tourism field are expected to be employed by the department. There is only one (1) administrative employee in the department.

The department excels in the fields of integrated marketing communications, services marketing, entrepreneurship, tourism, and general marketing. Members of the Department perform independent or collaborative research and consultations with partners from both inside and outside the nation, covering a wide range of industries such as retailing, branding, advocacy, services marketing, customer service, entrepreneurship and company development, environmental preservation, tourism, international trade, and international business.

The department has initiated conversations with PBZ, Azania, and CRDB banks in addition have signed a memorandum of understanding with Tanzania Women Architects for Humanity (TAWAH). DUMA and TIA collaborate with the department through the Marketing Students' Association (ZUMA).
