Background Information

The Zanzibar University, the first University on the Isles, is a private institution sponsored by Darul Iman Charitable Association (DICA). The main campus is situated at Tunguu area, in the Central District, some 19 kilometers from Zanzibar Town. The University campus, with a total area of 69 hectares of land, is located among pleasant and quiet countryside surroundings overlooking vast expanses of deep blue waters of Indian Ocean. It is an ideal place for serious academic work and research.

Ownership And Accreditation

The Zanzibar University was founded and is owned and governed by Darul-Iman Charitable Association. It was established on the basis of: the Constitution of Darul-Iman (1996), an Interim Authority from HEAC (1998), and Full Registration in 2000. The Charter was issued by the President of the United Republic of Tanzania in 2010.

Our Vision & Mission

Guiding principles that shape our journey

Our Vision

The vision of the Zanzibar University is to become a Centre of Excellence for the acquisition and dissemination of knowledge through teaching, research and consultancy with the view of providing expertise to the appropriate wider markets within Tanzania and beyond by the year 2025.

Our Mission

The mission of the University is to educate broadly and liberally men and women without discrimination of race, religion or physical disability, so as to reduce the severe inadequacy of qualified professionals and practitioners, improve the quality of education with the ultimate aim of up lifting the quality of life of Tanzanians in particular, and that of other people at large.

Director's Welcome Message

(Daral Iman)

It is with great honor and profound responsibility that I welcome you to Daral Iman Charitable Association (DICA). Our work is driven by the belief that every individual deserves the opportunity to live with dignity and hope based on values and ethics. As a beacon of compassion and progress, DICA is dedicated to uplifting communities through a comprehensive approach that integrates educational excellence, vocational training, essential services, charitable work, and community engagement.

It is worth mentioning that DICA’s goals are aligned with national broader development aspirations - Zanzibar's long-term Development Vision (ZDV 2050). Moreover, in line with international development including the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), serious measures are to be taken to uplift the human capacity as an engine for the development process as well as the African Union (AU) Agenda 2063. Furthermore, DICA collaborates closely with other like-minded institutions locally, regionally, and internationally.

Together, we can transform lives, strengthen communities, and pave the way for sustainable development and lasting change. We invite you to join us in this noble endeavor. Your support as a partner, advocate, or contributor is essential to building a brighter, more equitable future for all.

Background Information

(Daral Iman)

Daral Iman Charitable Association (DICA) was registered under the Society's Act No. 6, 1995 on August 2, 1996 (Registration No: Z0000113778) in Zanzibar, where its headquarters is located. DICA aims to achieve several goals, depending on the resources of charitable work and the response of the host countries.
Consistently with its objectives and following the agreement signed with the Revolution Government of Zanzibar, on January 11, 1998, DICA established Zanzibar University (ZU) as an endowment university.
The association is the founder, owner, sponsor, and main financier of the university’s activities and infrastructure, as well as the sponsorship of students from funding sources, endowments, and donations of philanthropists and donors from all over the Islamic world.

Vision

Empowering future generations through education, support programs, and charitable work.

Mission

DICA focuses on offering top-notch education and sustainable charity programs that improve lives and uplift communities.

Our Objectives
  1. To promote values, ethics, and education in Zanzibar and Africa, by relying on income from institutions, investments, commercial projects, charitable work resources, and the engagement of host countries.

  2. To manage (or run schools), educational and vocational institutions, and improve their conditions.

  3. To establish cultural, research, and rehabilitation units that prioritize specializations and their development, focusing on creative management, inventions, and other areas of interest to society.

  4. To share knowledge, and educate people through meetings, conferences, symposiums, exhibitions, publications, and public lecturers, while respecting the community and the laws of the host country.

  5. To establish scholarships and financial aid programs to help students from low-income families pursue higher education.

  6. To create and maintain community centers, vocational training, and skill development programs to help community members secure employment and improve their economic status.

  7. To implement initiatives that promote sustainable community development and overall well-being, including health, nutrition, environmental education, and practices.

  8. To build partnerships with local businesses, government agencies, and other similar organizations to maximize the impact of educational and community development efforts.

  9. To carry out any other work which is compatible with or incidental to the general purposes of the Organization and does not conflict with the provisions of this constitution.
Our Activities

ZU Students Sponsorship:
DICA continued to provide sponsorships to 694 students in various academic programs according to the three categories (Scholarship, Discount, and Loan).

Medical Services:
Year after year, the association continued to provide free-of-charge medical services and health care to the students (3,184), academic and non-academic employees (226) of ZU, through Al-Zahraa Dispensary. Moreover, it provides free medical care services including examination, diagnosis, and laboratory screenings to the surrounding community (not less than 1,200 persons), with a nominal fee for medication.

Students Iftar during Ramadhan:
DICA continued its efforts of supporting Ramadhan Iftar for ZU students in the hostels throughout the month of Ramadhan,1444, by providing free meals (Iftar and suhoor) for all of those who are fasting and residing in the hostel.

Arabic Language Program:
In an effort to empower the Zanzibari community and expand opportunities, DICA provided a free program to all those interested and dedicated to learning the Arabic language, including ZU employees, students, and the local community. The total of students registered in this program is around 150 students in the main campus (Tunguu) and Mpendae.

Sports Activities:
DICA is interested in sponsoring sports activities at ZU to cultivate students' talents and further their physical vigor and mental faculties and it allocates a significant budget to it. ZU team is considered the first in football and basketball. A gymnasium was established at the university, equipped with all sports equipment and devices, to provide its services to students, the university, and the neighboring community, at nominal prices.

Alzahraa Mosque Community:
The Association is considered the official sponsor of all activities of the University Mosque, as it is considered one of the largest mosques in the region. It has a major role in spreading Islamic culture and serving the Islamic community by providing lectures and religious lessons as well as Holy Quran competitions. Moreover, it has a special program in coordination with the relevant authorities to collect and distribute zakat and charity (Sadaqa).

Governance

Corporate Governance: The Association is committed to the highest standards of corporate governance. Its governance structure is flexible enough to adapt the changes in the internal and external environment. DICA strives to regularly review its processes, rules, regulations, and structures to ensure the best performance.
